What is Flat Top Grilling?

Flat top grilling is a type of cooking that involves using a flat, solid cooking surface to prepare food.

This surface is typically made of steel or cast iron and is heated by gas or electric burners located underneath.

Unlike traditional grilling, which involves cooking food over an open flame, flat top grilling provides a flat, even cooking surface that allows for more precise temperature control and a wider range of cooking techniques.

Flat top grilling is commonly used in restaurants and food trucks to prepare a variety of dishes, including burgers, pancakes, stir-fry, and even seafood.

The flat surface allows for easy flipping and turning of food, and the lack of grates means that smaller items won't fall through the cracks.

Also, the flat top surface allows for the use of pots and pans, making it a versatile cooking method for a variety of dishes.

One of the benefits of flat top grilling is that it allows for a healthier cooking method. The flat surface means that excess fat and grease can easily be drained away from the food, resulting in a healthier final product.

Additionally, the lack of open flames means that there is less risk of flare-ups and burning, which can also contribute to a healthier cooking method.

How to Season a Flat Top Grill

Oil Choice for Griddles

Seasoning a flat top grill is essential to ensure that it lasts longer and cooks food evenly. The first step in seasoning a flat top grill is to choose the right oil.

Many oils can be used to season a flat top grill, but some are better than others. The most commonly used oils for seasoning a flat top grill are vegetable oil, canola oil, and flaxseed oil.

Vegetable oil is a popular choice for seasoning a flat top grill because it has a high smoke point and is readily available.

Canola oil is another popular choice because it is affordable and has a neutral flavor.

Flaxseed oil is the best choice for seasoning a flat top grill because it creates a hard, non-stick surface. However, it is more expensive and can be difficult to find.

When choosing an oil to season a flat top grill, it is important to avoid oils with low smoke points, such as olive oil, as they can burn and create a sticky residue on the grill surface.

Steps to Season a Flat Top Grill

Once you have selected the oil of your choice, you can begin seasoning your flat top grill. Follow these simple steps:

  1. Heat the grill to around 300°F – 350°F (150°C – 175°C) for 30 minutes.
  2. While the surface is slightly warm, apply a small amount of oil to the surface of the grill using a paper towel or a brush. Be sure to spread the oil evenly over the entire surface of the grill.
  3. Turn off the heat and let the grill cool down completely.
  4. Repeat steps two and three several times until the grill surface is dark and shiny.

It is important to note that seasoning a flat top grill is not a one-time process. You should season your grill regularly to maintain its non-stick surface and prevent rusting.

How to Cook on a Flat Top Grill

Griddle Heat Zone and How To Use Them To Your Advantage

Flat top grills have a large, flat cooking surface that allows for even heating and cooking. One of the key advantages of a flat top grill is the ability to use different heat zones to cook different foods at the same time.

The heat zones on a flat top grill are typically divided into three sections: the hot zone, the warm zone, and the cool zone.

The hot zone is located at the center of the grill and is the hottest part of the cooking surface. This is where you can sear meats, cook pancakes, and other foods that require high heat.

The warm zone is located on the outer edges of the hot zone and is slightly cooler. This is where you can keep cooked foods warm while you finish cooking other items.

Finally, the cool zone is located on the outer edges of the grill and is the coolest part of the cooking surface. This is where you can keep foods warm without cooking them further.

To use the heat zones to your advantage, start by preheating the grill on high for 10-15 minutes. Then, adjust the heat as needed to create the desired temperature zones.

For example, if you want to cook pancakes in the hot zone and keep bacon warm in the warm zone, turn down the heat in the warm zone while leaving the hot zone on high.

This will allow you to cook both items at the same time without burning the bacon or undercooking the pancakes.

Cooking Tips and Techniques

When cooking on a flat top grill, there are a few tips and techniques that can help you achieve the best results:

  • Don't preheat on high. Preheating on high can cause the grill to become too hot, which can lead to uneven cooking and burnt food. Instead, preheat on medium-high and adjust the heat as needed.
  • Clean the griddle as you go with a scraper. This will prevent food from sticking to the surface and make cleanup easier.
  • Oil your gas griddle surface after every cook. This will help prevent rust and keep the surface non-stick.
  • Don't rely on your heat zones alone. Use other cooking techniques like flipping, stirring, and moving food around the surface to ensure even cooking.

Maintenance and Cleaning of a Flat Top Grill

Grease Disposal

One of the most important aspects of maintaining a flat top grill is proper grease disposal. After each use, it is essential to remove all grease and debris from the grill's surface.

This can be done using a scraper or spatula to push the grease into the grease trough. Once the grease has been collected in the trough, it can be disposed of in a safe and environmentally friendly way.

It is important to note that grease should never be poured down a drain or disposed of in a regular trash can.

Re-Oil After Every Cook

After each use, it is also important to re-oil the grill's surface. This helps to prevent rust and keep the grill in good condition.

To re-oil the grill, simply apply a thin layer of oil to the surface using a paper towel or cloth. Be sure to use a high smoke point oil, such as canola or vegetable oil, to prevent the oil from burning and creating smoke.

Rust Happens

Despite proper maintenance, rust may still occur on a flat top grill. If rust is present, it is important to address it as soon as possible to prevent further damage.

To remove rust from a flat top grill, use a rust remover specifically designed for use on grills. Apply the rust remover to the affected area and let it sit for the recommended amount of time.

Then, use a scraper or steel wool to remove the rust. Once the rust has been removed, be sure to re-oil the grill's surface to prevent further rust from forming.

Maintaining and cleaning a flat top grill is essential to ensure its longevity and optimal performance.
