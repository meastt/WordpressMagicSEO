Understanding the Science of Seasoning

Seasoning a cast iron griddle creates a non-stick surface that is also durable and easy to clean. The seasoning process involves heating the griddle to a high temperature and then applying a thin layer of oil. The oil polymerizes, or hardens, at high temperatures, creating a protective coating on the surface of the griddle.

Explanation of the Polymerization Process

Polymerization is a chemical reaction in which small molecules combine to form larger molecules. In the case of seasoning a cast iron griddle, the oil molecules combine to form a long chain of molecules. This chain of molecules is what creates the non-stick surface on the griddle.

Over time, the seasoning on a griddle can wear down. This can happen from cooking acidic foods, using metal utensils, or simply from repeated use.   • A stovetop
