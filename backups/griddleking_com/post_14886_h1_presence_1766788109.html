Breakfast Recipes Start your morning off right with a delicious flat top grill recipe! • Bacon and Egg Bread Bowls – Start by preheating your flat top grill to medium-high heat. Then, prepare a bread bowl by lightly toasting an un-sliced french bread loaf in the oven. Once toasted, cut off the top of the bread and scoop out the center, leaving an inch or two on the sides and bottom. Set aside the insides and make the filling: fry 2-3 strips of bacon on the flat top grill until crispy. Remove from the heat and set aside. Crack 4 eggs into the same plate and turn the heat to low. When the eggs are scrambled, add a handful of shredded cheese, crumbled bacon, and season with salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Stuff the eggs and bacon into the bread bowl and serve with warm maple syrup. • French Toast Skewers – Start by preheating your flat top grill to medium-high heat. Cut thick slices of French bread into cubes, skewer them on a kabob skewer, and brush with melted butter. Then, pour a mixture of 2 eggs, 1 cup of half and half, and a splash of vanilla extract in a shallow bowl. Dip the French bread cubes in the mixture and place them on the flat top grill. Fry until golden brown and serve with a sprinkle of cinnamon and your favorite toppings. • Breakfast Flatbread – Start by preheating your flat top grill to medium-high heat. In a small bowl, mix together 4 tablespoons of olive oil, ½ tablespoon of honey, 1 teaspoon of minced garlic, 1 teaspoon of thyme, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Spread the mixture onto a flatbread crust. Then, top with shredded cheese, diced tomatoes, diced onions, mushrooms, and chopped bacon. Place the flatbread on the flat top grill and cook for about 5 minutes. Carefully flip the flatbread and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the cheese melts. Slice into wedges and enjoy! Lunch and Dinner Recipes • Buffalo Turkey Burgers – Start by preheating your flat top grill to medium-high heat. Prepare a mixture of 1 lb ground turkey, ½ cup breadcrumbs, 1 egg, 1 teaspoon minced garlic, 1 teaspoon dried parsley, 1 teaspoon cumin, ¼ cup diced onion, and 1 tablespoon of hot sauce. Form the mixture into four patties, brush with a tablespoon of olive oil, and place on the hot flat top grill. Grill on each side for 6-8 minutes, or until cooked through. Serve with buns and your favorite toppings. • Grilled Chicken & Vegetable Bowls – Start by preheating your flat top grill to medium-high heat. Prepare 4 boneless and skinless chicken breasts by seasoning with 1 tablespoon of olive oil and your favorite seasonings. Place the chicken on the hot flat top grill and cook for 5-7 minutes per side, or until cooked through. Then, add 1 sliced zucchini, 1 sliced yellow squash, 1 diced red pepper, and 1 diced onion to the grill. Cook for 5-7 minutes, or until vegetables are slightly charred. Serve the grilled chicken and vegetables in bowls and top with a drizzle of ranch dressing. • Barbeque Pork Chops – Start by preheating your flat top grill to medium-high heat. Prepare 4 boneless pork chops by seasoning with 1 tablespoon of olive oil, ½ teaspoon of garlic salt, and 1 teaspoon of Italian seasoning. Place the pork chops on the hot flat top grill and cook for 5-7 minutes per side, or until cooked through. Then, brush the pork chops with your favorite barbeque sauce and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes. Serve the barbeque pork chops with your favorite side dishes. Dessert Recipes • Peaches and Cream Grilled Waffle – Start by preheating your flat top grill to medium-high heat. Cut four pre-made waffles into quarters and place on hot flat top grill. Grill for 2-3 minutes, or until lightly toasted. Then, halve 4 peaches and add them to the hot flat top grill. Grill for 3-4 minutes, or until tender. Place the toasted waffles on a plate and top with the grilled peaches and a scoop of vanilla ice cream. Drizzle with honey and enjoy! • S’mores Quesadillas – Start by preheating your flat top grill to medium-high heat. Place a large burrito-sized tortilla on the hot flat top grill and bake for 3-4 minutes, or until lightly toasted. Flip the tortilla and top it with marshmallow cream, chocolate chips, and mini marshmallows. Once the marshmallows begin to melt, fold the tortilla in half like a quesadilla and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes, or until lightly toasted. Serve with a dusting of powdered sugar and your favorite dipping sauces. • Grilled Pineapple Sundaes – Start by preheating your flat top grill to medium-high heat. Cut a pineapple into 8 slices and brush with 1 tablespoon of melted butter. Place the pineapple slices on the hot flat top grill and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until lightly charred. Place two grilled pineapple slices in a bowl and top with two scoops of your favorite ice cream. Drizzle with caramel sauce and serve with a sprinkle of cinnamon. Tips & Tricks • When grilling, always preheat your flat top grill to the desired temperature before placing the food on. • Make sure to oil or butter the grill when grilling delicate items such as fish or vegetables to prevent sticking. • When grilling meats, make sure to cook until the desired internal temperature has been reached to ensure food safety. • When grilling sweet items such as fruit and dessert, sprinkle with a small amount of sugar before grilling to enhance the natural sweetness. • Have fun!
