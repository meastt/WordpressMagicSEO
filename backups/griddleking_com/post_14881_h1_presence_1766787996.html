The Best Flat Top Grill Oils for Tasty Results When it comes to outdoor cooking, oils are essential for a delicious, juicy and tender result. When looking for the best flat top grill oils for tasty results, you should consider a number of factors. First and foremost, oil should have a high smoking point, meaning that it should have a low risk of burning or smoking on contact with the hot metal surface of the flat top grill. Additionally, different oils offer different ways to enhance and flavor the dishes you are grilling. Some oils add wonderful smoky notes, while others add sweetness and depth of flavor. Types of Oils to Use on Your Grill When it comes to choosing the best type of cooking oil for your flat top grill, there are a few different considerations that you should keep in mind. This includes the smoke point, flavor, and health benefits. The oil you choose will depend on what type of food you are grilling and what flavors you want to enhance. Here are some of the most popular and best flat top grill oils: • Canola oil: Canola oil is an all-purpose cooking oil that is popularly used in a number of cooking applications. It has a neutral flavor and is made from the pressed seeds of a rapeseed plant. It is also an excellent oil for grilling; it has a high smoke point of 400 degrees Fahrenheit and won’t burn or smoke. • Olive oil: Another popular option for flat top grills is extra virgin olive oil. It is a type of monounsaturated fat that gives food a buttery flavor and is also said to have health benefits. Olive oil has a low smoke point, however, so it’s best to use a lower temperature when cooking with it. • Coconut oil: Coconut oil is a great choice for grilled foods because it is known for its silky texture and nutty flavor. It has a very high smoke point of 450 degrees Fahrenheit, so it is less likely to burn or smoke. • Ghee: Ghee is a type of clarified butter that is a great choice for grilling. It has a high smoke point of 485 degrees Fahrenheit and is said to provide a rich and buttery flavor to grilled foods. • Avocado oil: Avocado oil has a mild, nutty flavor and is a great choice for grilling. It has a high smoke point of 520 degrees Fahrenheit and is said to be nutritious and beneficial to your health. Benefits of Using Different Oils Different oils offer a variety of benefits beyond just flavor. There are significant health benefits associated with certain oils, as well as various reasons why you might choose one oil over another. For example, some oils are known to be higher in healthy fats like monounsaturated fats and omega-3 fatty acids. Others are high in antioxidants and vitamins. Additionally, different oils have different smoke points, meaning that some may be more suitable for high temperature cooking, while others are better for low to medium temperature cooking.
