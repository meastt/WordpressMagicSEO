Welcome to the ultimate guide to sanding your Blackstone Griddle! As a fellow griddle enthusiast, I feel obligated to provide you with all the details necessary to make sure your griddle is in tip-top shape. But before we jump into the actual sanding process, I think it's important to take a step back and discuss why sanding your griddle is so important. When you’re talking about blackstone griddles, the need for sanding is particularly important. Why? Well, when you don’t sand the surface of a blackstone griddle, cooked-on particles and debris stay stuck to the surface, resulting in a dull and uneven finish. Plus, this debris that’s left behind can make it difficult to cook food properly. In order to prevent this from happening and keep your griddle looking and performing its best, you need to regularly sand the surface. This straightforward process will remove any of the debris and restore the surface of your griddle to a smooth and even finish. Now that you know why sanding the griddle is important, let's move on to the practical steps you need to take in order to complete the process. So, let’s get started! Now that you’re ready to begin, the first step is to heat up your griddle. This is important because sanding cold griddle leads to a lot of clogs, making the job a lot harder (and messier) than it has to be. A higher temperature also produces a better finish since it’s easier to remove the tough debris stuck to the surface. Once your griddle has been heated up to at least 300°F (150°C), it’s time to grab your sanding tools. The most common tool for sanding blackstone griddles is an 80-grit sandpaper, but you can also use a nylon scouring pad or wire brush if you prefer. Make sure to avoid tools with sharp edges, as these can damage the surface of your griddle. Once you’ve got the right tools, it’s time to start sanding. Begin by lightly sanding the entire surface using a circular motion. Make sure to apply just enough pressure to get rid of the tough debris while avoiding any deep gouges in the griddle’s surface. After you’ve finished sanding the surface, it’s time to clean up. Wipe down the griddle with a damp rag or newspaper to remove any leftover dust. You can also use a product like Griddle Buddy to help remove the last bit of debris. Finally, it’s time to season your griddle! Use an even coating of vegetable oil, let it sit for 15-30 minutes, and then wipe off the excess with a paper towel. Part of the trick is to put the oil on your griddle when it’s still warm, as this helps the seasoning to properly set. Congratulations! You’ve successfully completed the sanding process and restored your griddle to its former glory. But just because you’ve sanded your griddle doesn’t mean you’re done. In order to maintain a smooth and even surface and keep your griddle performing its best, here are a few tips to help: - Clean your griddle after every use. Pay particular attention to the cooking surface, which should be scraped, wiped down, and repeatedly oiled. - Avoid using metal utensils, as these can scratch and damage the surface. Instead, opt for wooden or plastic utensils that won’t cause any damage. - Finally, to avoid any rusting, make sure to store and handle your griddle correctly. This means always wiping down the surface after use and never submerging your griddle into water. That's it! You're now a Blackstone Griddle expert. Hopefully, this guide has helped you learn the ropes and provided you with everything you need to know to keep your griddle in perfect condition. So, go forth and cook on!
