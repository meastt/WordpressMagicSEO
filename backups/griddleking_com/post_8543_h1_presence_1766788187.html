No Seasoning Needed: Cast Iron vs Ceramic Griddle Cooking Surfaces

One of the biggest differences you might notice when shopping for griddles, is Pit Boss uses a ceramic coated griddle surface they call the “Proprietary non-stick armored ceramic cooking surface”. 

It should be noted that the non-stick ceramic armored coated griddle is only available on the Ultimate Series of griddles offered by Pit Boss. 

If you have the Standard Series of griddles, you will see the same steel griddle top commonly found in most griddles. 

Pit Boss is the first griddle brand I have seen (at least with outdoor griddles) that does not require a seasoning process to achieve a non-stick cooking surface. 

According to Pit Boss, this new ceramic cooking surface is ready to roll right out of the box and will last longer as it will not rust.

Now, with that in mind, you will need to use ceramic safe cooking utensils. 

If you were to use standard metal cooking utensils, you would scratch the ceramic coating and ultimately it would ruin the ceramic coating and its non-stick ability.

Is Pit Boss or Blackstone Better?

As someone who owns several Blackstone Griddles, I must admit that the new Pit Boss Ultimate Series is a better griddle than the current offerings from Blackstone.

What is the Pit Boss Griddle top made of?

In the standard series Pit Boss Griddle, the griddle top is a 4.7mm thick steel griddle surface. In the Pit Boss Ultimate Griddle series, the griddle top is a ceramic coated, non-stick surface. 

Can You Use Metal on a Pit Boss Griddle

Yes, you can use metal on the Standard series griddles from Pit Boss.

On the other hand, if you have the Ultimate Lift-off series, that ceramic coating cannot withstand metal utensils and will require you to buy ceramic safe utensils.
