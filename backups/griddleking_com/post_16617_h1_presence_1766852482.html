Blackstone griddles are a popular choice for outdoor cooking enthusiasts. They are versatile, easy to use, and can cook a variety of foods.

However, over time, the surface of the griddle can become rough and uneven, making it difficult to cook on. This is where sanding comes in.

Sanding the surface of the griddle can help to smooth out any rough spots and make it easier to cook on. But is sanding a good idea? In this article, we will explore the pros and cons of sanding a Blackstone griddle.

What is Sanding?

Sanding is the process of using an abrasive material to smooth out a surface.

In the case of a Blackstone griddle, sanding involves using sandpaper or a sanding disc to remove any rough spots or imperfections on the surface of the griddle.

Sanding can be done by hand or with a power tool, such as an angle grinder or a sander.

Why Sand a Griddle or Flat Top Grill?

Sanding a griddle or flat top grill is an important step in maintaining its surface. Over time, these cooking surfaces can become scratched or pitted, which can lead to uneven cooking and a buildup of food particles that are difficult to remove.

Sanding the surface with a fine grit sandpaper can help to smooth out these imperfections and create a more even cooking surface.

This improves the quality of the food being cooked and makes it easier to clean and maintain the griddle or grill.

Sanding can also help to remove any rust or discoloration that may have developed on the surface, ensuring that it continues to look and perform its best for years to come.

The Pros of Sanding a Blackstone Griddle

Before you write off the idea of sanding your griddle cooking surface as a "dumb idea", there are several benefits to sanding a Blackstone griddle. These include:

Improved Cooking Surface

Sanding the surface of the griddle can help to remove any rough spots or imperfections, making it easier to cook on. A smooth surface will allow food to cook evenly and prevent it from sticking to the griddle.

Extended Lifespan

Sanding can help to extend the lifespan of a Blackstone griddle. By removing any rough spots or imperfections, the griddle will be less likely to develop rust or other forms of corrosion.

This can help to prolong the life of the griddle and save you money in the long run.

Improved Appearance

Sanding can also improve the appearance of a Blackstone griddle. Over time, the surface of the griddle can become discolored or stained.

Sanding can help to remove these stains and restore the griddle to its original appearance.

The Cons of Sanding a Blackstone Griddle

While there are several benefits to sanding a Blackstone griddle, there are also some potential drawbacks. These include:

Voided Warranty

Sanding a Blackstone griddle can void the manufacturer's warranty. If you sand your griddle and it becomes damaged or malfunctions, you may not be able to get it repaired or replaced under warranty.

Difficult to Sand

Sanding a Blackstone griddle can be a difficult and time-consuming process. The surface of the griddle is large and uneven, which can make it challenging to sand evenly. Additionally, if you are sanding by hand, it can be tiring and hard on your hands.

Potential Damage

Sanding a Blackstone griddle can also potentially damage the surface of the griddle. If you use too much pressure or sand too aggressively, you can scratch or gouge the surface of the griddle.

This can make it even more difficult to cook on and can also lead to rust or other forms of corrosion.

Alternatives to Sanding

If you are hesitant to sand your Blackstone griddle, there are some alternatives that you can try. These include:

Seasoning

Seasoning is the process of coating the surface of the griddle with oil and heating it to create a non-stick surface.

This can help to smooth out any rough spots or imperfections on the surface of the griddle and make it easier to cook on.

Cleaning

Regular cleaning can also help to keep your Blackstone griddle in good condition.

Use a scraper or spatula to remove any food debris or grease from the surface of the griddle after each use.

You can also use a degreaser or other cleaning solution to remove any stubborn stains or buildup.

Replacement

If your Blackstone griddle is beyond repair, you may need to consider replacing it. While this can be expensive, it may be the best option if your griddle is no longer functioning properly or is too damaged to be repaired.

Conclusion

Sanding a Blackstone griddle can be a good way to improve its cooking surface and extend its lifespan.

However, there are also some potential drawbacks to consider, such as voiding the warranty and the potential for damage.

If you are hesitant to sand your griddle, there are some alternatives that you can try, such as seasoning and regular cleaning.

Ultimately, the decision to sand your Blackstone griddle will depend on your personal preferences and the condition of your griddle.
