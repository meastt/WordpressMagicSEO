What to Look For When Choosing a Small Charcoal Grill

Size

I suppose this is obvious, but hear me out. When it comes to size, there are a few different things to consider when choosing a small charcoal grill.

First, consider the overall size of the grill itself. Small charcoal grills are typically designed to be portable and lightweight, so they don't take up much space and can be easily moved around.

However, you'll want to make sure that the grill you choose will fit comfortably in the space where you plan to use it. This includes not only the footprint of the grill, but also the height of the grill and any additional features, such as a side table or a built-in thermometer.

Another aspect of size to consider is the cooking surface of the grill. This is typically measured in square inches, and for a small charcoal grill, it ranges around 150-300 square inches. This will give you a good idea of how much cooking space you'll have available.

Consider the number of people you'll typically be cooking for as well. If you usually only cook for one or two people, then a smaller cooking surface may be sufficient.

However, if you frequently entertain or cook for larger groups, you may want to consider a grill with a larger cooking surface.

Finally, you should also consider the size of the food you'll be cooking on the grill. If you plan to mostly cook small items such as burgers, hotdogs or kabobs, then a smaller cooking surface may be sufficient.

But if you plan to cook larger items such as steaks, chicken or fish, you'll want to look for a grill with a larger cooking surface.

Cooking Surface

The cooking surface of a charcoal grill is the area on which you place your food to cook. It's typically measured in square inches, and for a small charcoal grill, it ranges around 150-300 square inches.

When choosing a grill, it's important to consider how much cooking space you'll need for your typical meals. For example, if you typically cook for one or two people, then a smaller cooking surface of around 150-200 square inches may be sufficient.

However, if you frequently entertain or cook for larger groups, you may want to consider a grill with a larger cooking surface of 250-300 square inches or more.

Another factor to consider is the shape of the cooking surface. Some grills have round or oval cooking surfaces, while others have rectangular or square surfaces.

The shape of the cooking surface can affect how much space you have for cooking and how much food you can fit on the grill at one time.

You also want to consider the material the cooking surface is made of. Stainless steel and porcelain-coated cast iron are popular options because they are durable, easy to clean and they distribute heat evenly.

Cast iron is particularly good at retaining heat, so it can be great for high heat cooking like searing. However, they can be heavy and they rust if not properly seasoned or stored.

Another important aspect is how well it's constructed, is it well-made, sturdy and well-designed. You want to make sure that the cooking grate is securely attached and that the grill is easy to assemble and disassemble for cleaning.

Durability

When it comes to choosing a small charcoal grill, durability is a key factor to consider. Not only does a durable grill have a longer lifespan and withstand regular use, it also maintains its performance over time.

Investing in a more durable grill can ultimately save money in the long run, as it will likely need to be replaced less frequently.

Two great options for durable small charcoal grills are NOMAD and PK Grills. Both companies make their grills from thick 100% aluminum, which means they will never rust. Additionally, Weber Smokey Joe has ceramic paint coating which not only improve the look but also a good protection to the grill and makes it last longer.

This makes both NOMAD and PK Grills, as well as Weber Smokey Joe, a great choice when picking a small charcoal grill.

Ventilation

Venting matters when using a charcoal grill because it allows you to control the airflow and temperature inside the grill. The vents, usually located at the bottom and on the lid, enable you to regulate the amount of oxygen that reaches the charcoal.

By adjusting the vents, you can increase or decrease the heat inside the grill, allowing you to cook at a wide range of temperatures. This is important for cooking different types of food, such as slow-cooking a brisket or grilling a steak at high heat.

Additionally, proper venting also allows you to control the amount of smoke inside the grill, which can affect the flavor of the food. By adjusting the vents, you can reduce the amount of smoke for a milder flavor or increase it for a smokier taste.

Proper venting also help with the fuel efficiency, too much or too little airflow can cause the charcoal to burn too hot or too low, causing the food to burn or undercook.

Ease of use

Ease of use is an important consideration when picking a small charcoal grill because it can greatly impact the overall grilling experience.

A grill that is easy to use will make setting up, lighting, and maintaining the fire simple and straightforward. This allows you to spend less time on the logistics of grilling and more time enjoying the food and company.

Look for a grill with an easy-to-use design, such as a hinged cooking grate that makes it easy to add charcoal or a removable ash pan that makes cleaning up a breeze.

Small charcoal grills can range in price from less than $20 to several hundred dollars. Consider how much you're willing to spend and what features are most important to you.

Price can be an important factor to consider when buying a small charcoal grill, as it can impact the overall value of the purchase.

A lower-priced grill may be more budget-friendly, but it may not have the same features or build quality as a higher-priced model. It could also have a shorter lifespan or require more maintenance. On the other hand, a higher-priced grill may have more advanced features or be made with higher-quality materials, which can lead to a longer lifespan, better performance, and overall better value over time.

When making a decision based on price, it's important to consider the cost over time, not only the initial purchase price. A grill that is cheaper but needs to be replaced or has a short lifespan may end up costing more in the long run. In contrast, a more expensive grill that lasts longer and requires less maintenance can be more cost-effective in the long run.

Additionally, you should also consider the value of the grill comparing to the features it offers. A high price does not always mean it's better, also a low price does not always mean it's bad.

Features

Some small charcoal grills come with additional features, such as built-in thermometers, side tables, or wheels for easy movement. Consider whether any of these features would be useful to you.

Availability of Replacement Parts
