Sapporo Ribs 

A great place to kick things off is with some Sapporo ribs!

You’ll have seen different variations of these all over the internet, but this one is inspired by Finding Inspiration In Food.

You’ll need:

  • Pork ribs
  • Sapporo beer (of course)
  • Soy sauce
  • Brown sugar
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic powder
  • Smoked paprika

Start by making a delicious sauce by mixing 1 cup of soy sauce, 1 cup of brown sugar, and 1 Sapporo beer together in a saucepan.

Boil it until it is reduced by half and the flavor intensifies to form a sticky glaze. Strain and allow to cool. 

Next, form a Teriyaki rub by mixing sugar, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and smoked paprika in a bowl.

You can make this to taste, but start with around 1 teaspoon of each and then adjust to the flavors you prefer. 

Then, strip the meat and cover with the dry rub.

If serving with your Yak grill, you’ll want to cook these for 2-3 hours at 250 degrees Fahrenheit first, and then allow guests to finish on the Yak grill.

The internal temperature should be 190 degrees before you finish on the Yak grill. 

Serve with the Sapporo Teriyaki sauce and a cool Sapporo beer on the side.

This Sapporo chili is also inspired by the folks over at Finding Inspiration In Food.

So all I’ll suggest is that if you typically add stock or water to your chili, reduce the amount by around 6oz, and replace it with half a bottle of Sapporo beer.

Serve it with a Sapporo beer, of course, because if you cook with it, you should drink with it too to really bring out the best flavors!
