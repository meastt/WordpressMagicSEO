Best Griddle Accessories

If you're the proud owner of a griddle, you may have already discovered the many delicious dishes that you can cook on it. However, even the best griddle doesn't come with all the accessories you might need to make the most of your cooking.

Some of the most useful accessories to consider include a spatula, a scraper, a grill press, and a thermometer. These items can help you flip and remove food, clean the griddle surface, press down on food for even cooking, and monitor the temperature of the griddle.

With these accessories, you'll be able to cook a variety of dishes with ease, from pancakes and eggs to burgers and steaks. While these items may not come with a new griddle, they are definitely worth considering to take your griddle cooking to the next level.


1. Temperature probe. This is an essential tool for griddle cooking. It's important to know the exact temperature of your griddle so you can quickly adjust it as needed for different recipes and ingredients. With a temperature probe, you can get an accurate reading of your griddle's temperature and track it as you cook.

2. Cookware. The right cookware can make all the difference when griddle cooking. You'll need sturdy yet lightweight cookware that won't damage the griddle's surface. Cast iron pans are generally ideal, as they handle high heat well and won't warp, but non-stick pans can be helpful too.

3. Cooking tools. Spatulas, scoops, and other kitchen tools are essential for making the most of your griddle cooking. Look for tools that are sturdy and won't scratch the griddle's surface, as well as those with comfortable handles.

4. Cleaning supplies. Keeping your griddle clean and safe is key, so it's important to have the right cleaning supplies and equipment on hand. This includes a griddle scrubber, paper towels, and a cleaning solution specifically designed for griddles. And always make sure to keep your griddle well-oiled to help preserve its finish.
5. Foil. Once your griddle is cooked, it's handy to have some foil nearby for wrapping the cooking surface to keep food warm until you're ready to serve. Just be sure to wait until the griddle has cooled before covering it with foil.

6. Grease catcher. Grease is an inevitable part of griddle cooking, so it's useful to have a separate grease catcher to keep your griddle's surface clean and safe. A grease catcher will also catch any small pieces of food that may fall off your food while cooking.

7. Diffuser plate. This is a helpful tool for diffusing intense heat and creating an even cooking surface on your griddle. Diffuser plates come in various shapes and sizes, so you should be able to find one that fits your griddle.

8. Grill stone or pizza stone. A grill stone or a pizza stone is great for griddle cooking pizza, flatbread, and other bread-based dishes. A stone distributes heat evenly and helps create a crispy crust.

9. Grease Dam. A grease dam is a helpful tool for cooking with lots of grease. They come in various shapes and sizes and fit around the edge of the griddle, trapping grease and keeping it away from the surface of the griddle, which helps keep it clean and safe.

10. Heat deflectors. When you need to quickly lower the temperature of your griddle, heat deflectors are the way to go. These will help disperse heat around the edges of the griddle, making it easier to manage and control the temperature.

Final bite

While some of these tools and accessories can be expensive, they're essential for proper griddle cooking. With the right griddle accessories, you can be sure that your griddle cooking is both safe and professional quality.
