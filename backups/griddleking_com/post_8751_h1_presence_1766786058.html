Importance of Knowing When Chicken on the Grill Is Done 

Summertime is grilling time, and chicken is an all-time favorite choice for the grill.

But before you throw those chicken breasts on the fire, it's important to know at what temperature the chicken is done.

Undercooked chicken can be unsafe to eat, while overcooked chicken can be dry and unappetizing. 

So what's the best way to tell when chicken is cooked through?

You will know your chicken is done when the internal temperature is 165 degrees Fahrenheit. This was pulled from the USDA, as the USDA recommends cooking chicken until it reaches an internal temperature of 165 degrees Fahrenheit.

You can use a meat thermometer to check the temperature of the chicken, or you can cut into the thickest part of the meat to check for doneness.

If the juices run clear and the meat is no longer pink, your chicken is ready to eat!

How to Grill Chicken Perfectly Every Time 

At what temperature is chicken done? The quick answer is at 165 degrees Fahrenheit.

But the best way to make sure your chicken is cooked all the way through is to use a meat thermometer.

You can find them at most kitchen stores or online. Chicken that's been properly grilled will be juicy and tender on the inside, with crispy, slightly charred skin on the outside.

Here's how to grill chicken perfectly every time.

First, start by preheating your grill to medium-high heat. If you are using a charcoal grill or cooking boneless and skinless chicken, the heat should be lower.

Then, prepare your chicken by patting it dry with paper towels and trimming any excess fat. 

Next, season the chicken as desired. You can’t go wrong with salt and pepper.

You can also use a store-bought seasoning blend or make your own using a combination of dried herbs and spices.

Once the chicken is seasoned, place it on the grill and cook it for five to seven minutes per side or until it reaches an internal temperature of 165 degrees Fahrenheit. 

Be sure to flip the chicken occasionally so that it doesn't stick to the grill and so that both sides have a chance to cook evenly.

And there you have it! Perfectly grilled chicken every time.

How Long Does It Take to Grill Chicken?

When it comes to grilling chicken, there is no one-size-fits-all answer.

The cooking time will vary depending on the size and thickness of the chicken, as well as the type of grill you are using.

However, there are a few general guidelines you can follow to ensure that your chicken is cooked through. 

For boneless, skinless chicken breasts, you should plan on grilling for five minutes per side.

If you are using bone-in chicken thighs or drumsticks, you may need to cook them for 10–12 minutes per side.

And finally, if you are grilling a whole chicken or roasting a chicken in the rotisserie, you will need to cook it for at least 20–30 minutes.

By following these basic guidelines, you can ensure that your chicken is cooked through and juicy every time.

How to Know When Chicken Is Done Without a Thermometer 

Any seasoned grill master knows that one of the most important skills to learn is how to tell when chicken is done cooking without a thermometer.

After all, nobody wants to serve undercooked poultry! 

Here are a few simple tips that will help you take your chicken from the grill to the dinner table with ease.

First, make sure that your chicken is cooked evenly by flipping it no more than every four minutes.

This will help to prevent overcooking on one side while the other remains raw. 

Second, make a small puncture in the meat and press down onto the meat.

Take a look at the juices running off of the meat; they should be clear, not pink or red.

Finally, give the meat a poke with a fork; it should feel firm to the touch but not hard.

With these guidelines in mind, you'll be grilling like a pro in no time!

The Importance of Letting Your Chicken Rest after Grilling

After you've grilled your chicken to perfection, it can be tempting to slice it right away.

However, it is important to resist this temptation and let the chicken rest for a few minutes before carving.

This gives the juices a chance to redistribute throughout the meat, making it juicier and more flavorful.

It also helps to ensure that the chicken is cooked all the way through. 

In case you didn’t know, meat of any kind will continue to cook for several minutes after being removed from the heat.

That means that if you cook your meat completely before removing it from the heat, by the time it's finished resting, it may be overcooked.

That’s why it’s important to let your chicken rest after taking it off the grill. 

By letting the chicken rest, you give the heat time to evenly penetrate the meat, ensuring that your meal is safe and delicious.

If you cut into it too soon, all of those juices will spill out and leave a tough, dry piece of meat behind.

So the next time you grill chicken, remember to let it rest before serving. Your taste buds will thank you!

Common Mistakes Beginners Make 

One of the most common mistakes beginners make when grilling chicken is not using a meat thermometer.

It’s important to cook chicken all the way through to prevent food poisoning, and the only way to ensure that it’s cooked properly is to use a meat thermometer or a trained eye (which most beginners don’t have). 

Another common mistake is failing to preheat the grill. This step is essential for ensuring that the chicken cooks evenly.

If you put your chicken on a cold grill, you won’t get that delicious crispy char on the outside, and it will take too long to cook.

Additionally, beginners often make the mistake of overcooking or undercooking the chicken.

Both of these problems can be avoided by using a meat thermometer and paying attention to the cooking time. 

Many beginners also make the mistake of not letting frozen chicken thaw completely before grilling.

If you put meat on the grill while the meat is still frozen, you will overcook the outside and be left with a cold, raw center—which nobody wants to eat.

With a little practice, anyone can master the art of grilling chicken.

By following these simple tips, beginners can avoid making common mistakes and enjoy delicious, perfectly cooked chicken every time.

Always Keep an Eye on the Grill

Every grill is a little bit different. Some grills have hot spots, which means that certain areas are hotter than others.

It’s important to keep an eye on the grill and move the chicken around so that it cooks evenly. 

This is especially important if you’re cooking for a large group of people because you don’t want anyone to end up with burnt or raw chicken.

By paying attention to the grill, you can avoid any disasters and ensure that everyone leaves the table happy and full.

Make sure the grill is properly heated before cooking the chicken.
